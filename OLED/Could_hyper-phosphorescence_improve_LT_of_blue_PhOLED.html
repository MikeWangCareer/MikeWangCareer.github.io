(1/4) PhOLED device lifetime (LT)

  • PhOLED device lifetime (LT) is determined by many factors (e.g., intrinsic stability, excited-state lifetime, charge balance, ...), among which the "color effect" is pronounced

  • Device LT of PhOLED: Green > sky-blue > blue (ranges are of different orders of magnitude)

  • Hyper-device type I (as depicted in figure a below), in general, should present (much) longer device lifetime (LT) than that of hyper-device type II (as depicted in figure b below), due to the different colors of ref devices (in sky-blue & purple)

(3/4) Fluorescent-emitter (FLUO)

  • A suitable fluorescent-emitter (FLUO) should be able to work with sky-blue or even greenish Phosphorescent-emitter (PHO) to achieve decent device LT

  • Certain favorable optical properties are required (e.g., intensive Abs., small Stokes shift, narrowband emission) to achieve efficient energy transfer (FRET)

  • v-DABNA (structure shown below) presents many desired optical properties, but the structure is too planar => low doping concentration in device & unfavorable Dexter transfer & undesired direct charge recombination => inefficient energy transfer (from PHO to FLUO) & unwanted triplet exciton on FLUO => color purity, efficiency & LT loss

  • Therefore, a more sterically shielded FLUO (than v-DABNA) with favorable optical properties (similar to v-DABNA) is desired for the application in blue PhOLED (with higher doping concentration than v-DABNA)

(4/4) Design rules

  • When attaching electron-donating group (EDG) or electron-withdrawing group (EWG) to phenyl ring, the ortho- & para-posiiton would be either electron-rich or electron-poor due to the resonance effect

  • In the case of v-DABNA, nitrogen atoms function as the the EDG due to the lone-pair electrons, while boron atoms as the EWG due to the empty p-orbital

  • These two nitrogen/boron atoms are meta to each other, so that the ortho- & para-positions are overlapped, which make these positions more electron-rich/poor

  • Nitrogen atoms are ortho to boron atoms. In this way, the electron-rich & poor positions are next to each other (i.e., alternating pattern), which is responsible for the favorable optical propeties (e.g., narrowband emission)


    • The "+" and "-" signs shown above stand for the electron-poor & electron-rich positions

  • Even with nitrogen atoms (EDG) only, narrowband emissoin could be achieved with a rigid core & two nitrogen atoms meta to each other

    • The structure with two nitrogen atoms para to each other shows broader & red-shifted emission spectrum compared with the structure with two nitrogen atoms meta to each other, therefore difficult to compare the FWHM in eV
