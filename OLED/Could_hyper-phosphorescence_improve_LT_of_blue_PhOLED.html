(1/4) Device lifetime (LT)

  • PhOLED device LT is influenced by many factors (e.g., intrinsic stability, excited-state lifetime, charge balance), among which the "color effect" is pronounced (see below)

  • Device LT of PhOLED: Green > sky-blue > blue (ranges are of different orders of magnitude)

(2/4) Approach

  • The "hyper approach" could take advantage of the "color effect", since sky-blue / greenish phosphorescent-emitter (PHO) could potentially be applied in blue PhOLED, as long as the fluorescent-emitter (FLUO) blue-shifts the emission properly (shown below)

Hyper-device type I (figure a) shows bluer emission than the ref device (w/o FLUO)

hyper-device type II (figure b) shows greener emission than the ref device (w/o FLUO)

(3/4) Requirements

  • Favorable optical properties (e.g., intensive Abs., small Stokes shift, narrowband emission) & relatively high doping concentration (> 2 wt%) of FLUO are desired in hyper-phosphorescence for efficient energy transfer (from PHO to FLUO) and mass production compatibility

  • v-DABNA (shown below), for example, presents many desired optical properties, but the structure is too planar => low doping concentration in device & unfavorable Dexter transfer & undesired direct charge recombination => inefficient energy transfer (from PHO to FLUO) & unwanted triplet exciton on FLUO => color purity, efficiency & device LT loss

  • Therefore, a sterically shielded FLUO with favorable optical properties & high doping concentration is required

(4/4) Design rules

  • It is known that when electron-donating group (EDG) or electron-withdrawing group (EWG) is attached to a phenyl ring, the ortho- & para-positons to the functional group (FG) on the phenyl ring would be electron-rich or electron-poor due to the "resonance"


  • In the case of v-DABNA, nitrogen atoms function as the EDG because of the lone-pair electrons, while boron atoms as the EWG due to the empty p-orbital


    • The "+" and "-" signs shown above stand for the electron-poor & electron-rich positions, respectively

    These two nitrogen/boron atoms are meta to each other, so that the ortho- & para-positions are overlapped, which make these positions more electron-rich/poor than the case with only one nitrogen/boron atom

    N is ortho/para to B. As a result, the electron-rich & poor positions are next to each other (alternating pattern), which is responsible for many favorable optical properties (e.g., narrowband emission, small Stokes shift)

  • Only with EDG, narrowband emission could also be achieved with: 1) rigid core; 2) nitrogen atoms meta to each other (as mICz shown below)

    • pICz (N-para-N) shows broader & greener emission than mICz (N-meta-N), but it is difficult to compare their FWHM values in eV based on the above image
